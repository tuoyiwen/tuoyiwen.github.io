This is my first blog post. I'm exploring how AI tools are reshaping the way we think about creativity at work.

## Why This Matters

As large language models become embedded in everyday workflows, the boundary between human creativity and machine assistance is blurring. What does it mean to be "creative" when your AI co-pilot can generate ideas, draft text, and suggest alternatives in seconds?

## What I'm Researching

My PhD work sits at the intersection of two fields:

- **Human-AI Interaction** — how people collaborate with AI systems
- **Employee Creativity** — what drives original and useful ideas in organizations

The question I keep coming back to: does AI amplify human creativity, or does it subtly constrain it?

More to come as my research unfolds.
